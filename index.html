We have an overview of the suggested dress code for each event below, as well as other tabs explaining the various types of Indian wear and where to procure it. A few guiding principles at the outset:

Indian Outfit Types Explained

For Women

Saree / Sari
The timeless classic

A long piece of beautiful fabric (typically 6 yards) draped and pleated elegantly, paired with a fitted blouse. A petticoat worn underneath provides structure to drape it on and is invisible once saree is draped. Fabrics range from silk to chiffon in countless colors.

You can opt for a pre-draped saree where the pleats are already set, and you just have to hook it on like a skirt. Otherwise, we will also provide draping help at the venue. Make sure you get a blouse and a petticoat when ordering your saree.

Lehenga
Long skirt + choli + dupatta

A three-piece ensemble: long flared skirt, a fitted crop top (choli), and a scarf (dupatta). Often heavily embellished. Wear the skirt higher on the waist or drape the dupatta in front for more coverage. Many ways to style — search YouTube or Pinterest for dupatta draping ideas.

Best for
All Events ✓✓
Anarkali Suit
Long flowing dress + churidar

A long, flowing dress with a fitted bodice and a flared skirt, usually paired with tapered pants (churidar). Graceful, movement-friendly, and elegant. Named after the legendary Mughal courtesan Anarkali — a truly timeless silhouette.

Best for
All Events ✓✓
Salwar Kameez
Tunic + loose pants + dupatta

A comfortable outfit with a tunic (kameez) and loose pants (salwar), paired with a dupatta. Slightly more casual than a saree or lehenga — but still beautiful and festive. Pants come as wide-legged palazzo or flared-from-the-knee sharara.

Best for
Haldi ✓✓
Indo-Western
The fusion option

A fusion of Western and Indian fashion — combining Western silhouettes with Indian ethnic details. Includes tunics with trousers, jumpsuits, cape sets, two-piece sets, and embroidered dresses. Perfect for guests who want a modern look while honoring the spirit of the celebration.

All Events ✓✓

For Men

Kurta Pajama
The classic go-to

A kurta (long tunic) worn with matching pants (pajama). Popular, comfortable, and versatile. Can be plain or embroidered for a festive look. Elevate easily with a stylish stole or scarf draped over one shoulder. Pair with loafers, dress shoes, or jutti.

Best for
Haldi (Preferred) ✓✓Other Events ✓
Nehru Jacket Set
Kurta + sleeveless vest

The Nehru jacket is the sleeveless vest worn on top of a kurta — it adds instant formality and polish to any look. Pair with trousers or churidar. One of the most versatile options for male guests across multiple events.

Best for
Sangeet ✓✓Wedding ✓✓
Jodhpuri / Bandhgala
The structured jacket

A jacket with a high collar and fitted silhouette — formal or semi-formal. Made from rich fabrics like silk, velvet, or brocade, often with intricate embroidery or decorative buttons. Paired with tailored trousers in a matching or complementary color for a polished look.

Best for
Sangeet ✓✓Wedding ✓✓
Sherwani
The most formal

A long coat-like garment worn over a kurta and churidar — the most formal of all men's Indian wear. Traditionally the groom's attire, but close family members and wedding party guests often also wear one to complement the groom's look.
